How I improved a landing page

Mar 2025 • WebEditz

1. Spacing
Good spacing makes a website clean, readable, and comfortable for users. Proper use of padding, margins, and white space helps separate sections, avoid clutter, and guide the user’s eyes smoothly through the page.
--- 2. Contrast
Contrast ensures that text and elements stand out clearly. Using strong color differences between background and text improves visibility, accessibility, and overall design quality. Good contrast helps users quickly understand the most important content.
--- 3. CTA Placement
CTA (Call-To-Action) buttons should be placed where users can easily notice and click them. Effective CTA placement includes putting buttons near important content, above the fold, and at the end of meaningful sections. The goal is to lead users toward actions like “Learn More,” “Contact Me,” or “Get Started.”

Tips for responsive typography

Feb 2025

1️⃣ Choose a Clean Font 1. Use simple and readable fonts like:

  • Poppins
  • Roboto
  • Inter
  • Open Sans
    • Avoid too many fancy fonts.
--- 2. Use Consistent Font Sizes
Create a clear hierarchy:
Heading 1: 32–48px
Heading 2: 24–32px
Paragraph: 14–18px
This makes your content easy to read. --- 3. Keep Line Spacing Comfortable
Use line-height: 1.4–1.7 This gives breathing room so text doesn’t look crowded.
--- 4. Maintain Proper Letter Spacing
Use slight spacing like: letter-spacing: 0.5px This increases clarity. --- 5. Use Strong Contrast
Dark text on a light background (or vice-versa). Never use light grey on white — looks weak and unreadable. --- 6. Limit to 2 Fonts Only
A common rule: One font for headings One font for body text Too many fonts look messy. --- 7. Keep Paragraphs Short
Large blocks of text look boring. Use short paragraphs with breaks. --- 8. Use Bold & Size, Not Colors
For highlighting, use: bold text bigger size Do not use random colors; it makes a website look unprofessional. --- 9. Align Text Properly
Left alignment is best for reading. Avoid center-aligned long paragraphs. --- 10. Add Enough Margin and Padding
Give space around text to avoid a cluttered layout.
